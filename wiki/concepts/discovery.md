# Discovery

## Definition

Discovery is the first of the three iterative Behaviour-Driven Development (BDD) practices. It focuses on exploring what the system could do through collaborative workshops, resulting in a shared understanding among team members. The practice involves structured conversation formats like Example Mapping and Event Storming. The output of Discovery is a shared understanding of the behaviour to be built, captured as concrete examples.

## Key Characteristics

- Starts with collaborative workshops to explore system behaviour.
- Emphasises that discovery is the right place to start for teams new to BDD.
- Automation without discovery yields limited value.
- Uses structured conversation formats such as [[concepts/example-mapping|example-mapping]] and [[concepts/event-storming|event-storming]].
- Produces concrete examples as the primary output.
- Builds shared understanding among all team members (business, development, testing).

## Applications

- Kicking off a new BDD initiative by focusing on discovery before automation.
- Agile refinement sessions to clarify requirements and edge cases.
- Team alignment workshops to ensure everyone agrees on the scope of a feature.
- Reducing ambiguity in user stories by turning them into concrete examples.
- Integrating with [[concepts/formulation|formulation]] and [[concepts/automation|automation]] to form the complete BDD cycle.
