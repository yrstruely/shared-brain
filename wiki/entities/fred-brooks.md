# Fred Brooks

## Basic Information
- Type: person
- Source: [[sources/bdd-cucumber-gherkin-reference_7dfdd4]]

## Description
Fred Brooks is a prominent figure in software engineering, best known for authoring "The Mythical Man-Month" and leading the development of the IBM System/360 family of computers. In the source document, he is quoted for the insight: "The hardest single part of building a software system is deciding precisely what to build." This statement is used to underscore a core principle of Behaviour-Driven Development (BDD)—that determining what to build is the most challenging and critical aspect of software development. His emphasis on the difficulty of specification and requirements gathering directly supports BDD's focus on collaborative discovery and conversation before building. Brooks' contributions to software engineering project management, particularly his observations on Brooks' law ("Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later"), remain widely studied. In this context, he serves as an authoritative voice to motivate BDD practices by highlighting where software projects most often fail.
